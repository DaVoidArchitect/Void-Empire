# VOID BREAKTHROUGH OWNERSHIP CHARTER

## Purpose

This charter defines how breakthroughs are credited, protected, and operationalized across VOID,
including contributions from employees, citizens, subnet collaborations, and partner entities.

## Scope

Applies to all breakthrough classes:

- conceptual insights
- algorithms and models
- architecture and RTL
- materials/process proposals
- validation methods
- deployment and production innovations

## Core Doctrine

1. **Ownership belongs to creators.**
2. **Protection is a company obligation.**
3. **Attribution is immutable and mesh-recorded.**
4. **Reward is proportional to verified impact.**
5. **Operational use requires reproducible evidence.**

## Attribution Mesh Lifecycle

Every breakthrough must have a record from:

1. **Origin** — idea declaration and contributor set.
2. **Validation** — experiments, simulation, formal proofs, or measurable tests.
3. **Integration** — how it merges into system architecture or product flow.
4. **Impact** — quantified utility across reliability, performance, economics, or citizen value.
5. **Stewardship** — maintenance owner and change history.

## Rights Matrix (Policy Draft)

- **Creator rights:** authorship, ownership recognition, and impact-linked rewards.
- **VOID rights:** mission-aligned operational and commercial license to deploy safely.
- **Citizen/subnet rights:** attribution and reward when contribution is validated.

## Governance and Dispute Resolution

1. Disputes are resolved by evidence trails, contribution logs, and timestamped records.
2. Ambiguous authorship defaults to joint attribution until adjudicated.
3. Proven fraud, erasure, or manipulation invalidates claims and triggers policy enforcement.

## Legal Note

This charter is a policy artifact and must be harmonized with jurisdiction-specific employment,
contract, IP, and securities law before external execution.
